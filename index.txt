15:T2390,London and heat have a complicated relationship. The city isn't built for it — the underground becomes a sauna, the parks fill up overnight, and the entire population seems to collectively forget how to dress for warm weather. But among the chaos, there's always a subset of Londoners who manage to look genuinely put-together in the heat: cool, considered, and somehow completely unruffled.

What's their secret? It comes down to a handful of key pieces — summer staples that manage to be both practical and polished, comfortable enough for a 30-degree day and stylish enough for wherever that day takes you. Here are the five heatwave essentials that London's chicest dressers are reaching for this weekend.

![ Every Chic Londoner Is Wearing One of These 5 Summer Heatwave Staples This Weekend ](/media/articles/every-chic-londoner-is-wearing-one-of-these-5-summer-heatwave-staples-this-weekend/01.avif)

## Why London Heatwave Dressing Is Its Own Category

Dressing for heat in London is genuinely different from dressing for heat in, say, Barcelona or Los Angeles. The city's architecture, transport system, and social calendar weren't designed with warm weather in mind, which means you need pieces that can handle a sweaty tube journey, an outdoor lunch, a gallery visit, and an evening in a beer garden — all in the same day.

The pieces that work best in this context share a few qualities: they're made from breathable natural fabrics, they have a relaxed but intentional silhouette, they transition easily between settings, and they look as good at the end of the day as they did at the beginning. That last point is particularly important in London, where a heatwave day tends to be long and unpredictable.

The emphasis on timeless staples over fleeting trends also reflects a broader shift in how fashion-conscious Londoners are approaching their wardrobes — less about what's new, more about what genuinely works.

## The 5 Heatwave Staples Every Chic Londoner Is Wearing

**1. The Linen Shirt Dress**
The linen shirt dress is arguably the single most versatile piece in a London summer wardrobe. It works belted or unbelted, with sandals or sneakers, dressed up with jewelry or kept completely minimal. The fabric breathes beautifully in the heat, and the shirt-dress silhouette is relaxed enough to be comfortable while structured enough to look intentional. Opt for a mid-length hem — knee to midi — for the most versatile result.

**2. Wide-Leg Linen Trousers**
Wide-leg linen trousers have become a genuine wardrobe staple for the style-conscious Londoner, and it's easy to see why. They're cool in the literal sense — the loose silhouette allows air to circulate — and they look polished in a way that shorts and skirts sometimes don't. Pair with a simple fitted top or a tucked-in linen shirt. Neutral tones (white, ecru, tan, slate) are the most versatile, but a bold color can work beautifully as a statement piece.

**3. The Slip Dress**
The slip dress is the after-dark heatwave essential. Lightweight, minimal, and inherently elegant, it transitions from afternoon to evening without requiring a change of outfit. This season's versions tend toward longer hems and more refined fabrics — silk, satin, or high-quality viscose — that feel genuinely luxurious rather than simply casual. Layer with a lightweight cardigan or denim jacket for cooler moments.

**4. The Oversized Linen Blazer**
It might seem counterintuitive to reach for a blazer in a heatwave, but the oversized linen blazer is one of London's most reliable summer pieces. Worn open over a simple dress or top, it adds structure and polish without adding warmth. It also solves the perennial London summer problem of air-conditioned interiors — offices, restaurants, galleries — that can feel genuinely cold even on the hottest days. Choose an unlined version in a breathable fabric for maximum comfort.

**5. Elevated Flat Sandals**
The right flat sandal is the foundation of a great London heatwave outfit. Not a basic flip-flop, but something with a considered design — leather straps, interesting hardware, a quality sole — that looks intentional and holds up through a full day of walking. This season's most popular options include minimalist strappy styles, embellished flats with beading or metallic details, and updated thong sandals in quality leather. Invest in a pair that's genuinely comfortable as well as stylish; in London, you will walk.

## The Broader Significance: Comfort and Chicness Are No Longer Opposites

What's particularly interesting about London's heatwave style is what it says about the evolution of fashion more broadly. The emphasis on breathable fabrics, relaxed silhouettes, and versatile pieces reflects a consumer base that has definitively moved past the idea that looking good requires being uncomfortable.

This is a meaningful shift. For years, the fashion industry operated on the implicit assumption that style came at a physical cost — tight fits, impractical shoes, fabrics that looked beautiful but felt miserable. The current direction challenges that assumption entirely. The pieces that are dominating London's summer streets right now are genuinely comfortable, and they look better for it. There's an ease and confidence in the way they're worn that no amount of discomfort could replicate.

The definition of "chic" is evolving, and London is at the forefront of that evolution. It's no longer about the label, the price tag, or the difficulty of the look. It's about how you carry yourself — and the right pieces make that infinitely easier.

## Practical Tips for Dressing Well in a London Heatwave

- **Prioritize natural fabrics.** Linen, cotton, and silk breathe far better than synthetics and will keep you significantly more comfortable in the heat. They also tend to look better as the day progresses — synthetics can look limp and tired; natural fabrics develop a pleasant lived-in quality.
- **Invest in a quality flat sandal.** London requires walking, and a sandal that blisters or breaks on a hot day will ruin everything. Spend a little more on a pair that's both beautiful and genuinely comfortable.
- **Keep a lightweight layer.** Air conditioning in London can be aggressive. A linen blazer, a light cardigan, or even a large scarf gives you the option to cover up without carrying something heavy.
- **Dress for the whole day.** Think about where you'll be from morning to evening and choose pieces that work across all of those settings. The slip dress, the linen shirt dress, and the wide-leg trouser all do this well.
- **Don't underestimate accessories.** A quality sunhat, a pair of good sunglasses, and a simple tote bag can elevate even the most basic heatwave outfit into something that looks genuinely considered.

## Conclusion

Dressing well in a London heatwave is an art form — but it's one that comes down to a handful of reliable pieces rather than any complicated formula. The linen shirt dress, wide-leg trousers, slip dress, oversized blazer, and elevated flat sandal are the building blocks of a summer wardrobe that's both comfortable and genuinely chic. Get these right, and you'll be the one who looks effortlessly put-together while everyone else wilts.

## FAQ

**What should I wear in a London heatwave?**
Prioritize breathable natural fabrics — linen, cotton, and silk are your best options. Key pieces include linen shirt dresses, wide-leg linen trousers, slip dresses, and lightweight flat sandals. An unlined linen blazer is also useful for navigating air-conditioned interiors.

**What are the best fabrics for hot weather in the UK?**
Linen is the gold standard for hot weather — it's breathable, lightweight, and gets softer with wear. Cotton is also excellent. Avoid synthetic fabrics like polyester, which trap heat and can feel uncomfortable in warm temperatures.

**How do Londoners dress for summer?**
London's style-conscious residents tend to favor relaxed but intentional pieces — linen sets, slip dresses, wide-leg trousers, and elevated flat sandals. The emphasis is on versatility, since a typical London summer day can span multiple settings and temperatures.

**What shoes are best for walking in London in summer?**
A quality flat sandal with good support is the most practical and stylish option. Look for leather straps, a cushioned footbed, and a sole that can handle a full day of walking. Avoid flip-flops with hard soles or anything without adequate support.

**Is it worth buying a linen blazer for summer?**
Yes — an unlined linen blazer is one of the most versatile summer pieces you can own. It adds polish to any outfit, solves the air-conditioning problem, and looks genuinely chic without adding warmth. Choose a relaxed, slightly oversized fit for the most effortless result.

## The 7 Summer Trends to Know

**1. Colorful Shoes**
Bold footwear is one of the easiest ways to inject personality into an outfit without overhauling your entire wardrobe. This summer, the most interesting shoe choices are happening in cobalt blue, terracotta, cherry red, and bright yellow. The formula is simple: keep the rest of the outfit neutral and let the shoes do the talking. A white linen dress with cobalt sandals. Cream trousers with a red mule. The contrast is striking and the execution is effortless.

**2. Woven Bags**
The woven bag trend has been building for a couple of seasons, and this summer it reaches its peak. Raffia totes, wicker clutches, basket-weave shoulder bags — these textures feel inherently summery and pair beautifully with natural fabrics like linen, cotton, and lightweight denim. They also tend to be more affordable than leather alternatives, making them an accessible way to update your accessories without significant investment.

**3. Tankinis**
The tankini is having a genuine moment, and it's easy to understand why. It offers the coverage of a one-piece with the practicality of a two-piece — easier to manage at the beach, more flattering for a wider range of body types, and available in a much broader range of styles than it was a decade ago. This season's versions are elevated: think structured tops with interesting necklines, quality fabrics, and sophisticated color palettes that look as good on a boat as they do poolside.

**4. Bold Color Blocking**
Color blocking — pairing two or more contrasting solid colors in a single outfit — is one of the most effective ways to look intentional and fashion-forward without complicated styling. This summer's palette leans into unexpected combinations: orange and pink, green and blue, yellow and red. The key is to commit fully and keep the silhouettes simple so the color does the work.

**5. Linen Everything**
Linen has been a summer staple for decades, but this season it's elevated to near-wardrobe-essential status. Linen sets, linen dresses, linen trousers, linen shirts — the fabric's natural texture and breathability make it ideal for warm weather, and its slightly rumpled quality gives it an effortless, lived-in appeal that feels very much of the moment.

**6. Flip-Flop Color Trends**
The humble flip-flop is getting a serious upgrade this summer. Rather than the standard black or tan, the most interesting versions come in unexpected colors — dusty rose, sage green, bright white — with elevated materials and more refined silhouettes. They're the kind of shoe that looks intentional rather than like an afterthought, and they work with everything from a maxi dress to a pair of tailored shorts.

**7. Statement Swimwear Prints**
Beyond the tankini, swimwear this summer is embracing bold prints in a big way. Floral, abstract, geometric — the prints showing up on swimsuits and bikinis this season are genuinely interesting, the kind you'd want to wear as a top or skirt if you could. The best approach is to treat your swimwear as you would any other outfit: choose a print you genuinely love and style around it.

## Why These Trends Matter Beyond the Season

The playful direction of summer 2026 fashion isn't just a seasonal blip — it signals something more lasting about where consumer preferences are heading. After years of restraint, people want to enjoy getting dressed again. They want pieces that reflect their personality, spark conversation, and make them feel something when they put them on.

This is good news for the fashion industry, which has been navigating a complicated landscape of sustainability concerns, shifting consumer values, and the ongoing tension between trend cycles and timeless dressing. A woven bag you love and use for five summers is a better choice than three disposable alternatives.

- **Think about what you already own.** The best trend pieces are the ones that work with your existing wardrobe. Before buying, ask yourself: what three things do I already own that this would work with?
- **Don't be afraid of color.** If you've been defaulting to neutrals, summer is the ideal time to experiment. Start with a single colorful accessory — a bag, a shoe, a scarf — and see how it changes the energy of your outfits.

## The Cultural Shift Toward Personalization

The rise of custom clothing isn't happening in a vacuum. It's part of a broader cultural movement that values individuality, authenticity, and intentionality in the way we dress. For years, fast fashion dominated by offering trend-forward pieces at accessible prices — but the trade-off was homogeneity. Walk into any city center on a Saturday and you'd see the same five outfits repeated endlessly.

Custom clothing offers the antidote. Whether it's hand-painted denim, embroidered jackets, made-to-measure trousers, or screen-printed tees, bespoke garments give wearers something that mass production simply cannot: a piece that reflects who they actually are, not who a trend algorithm decided they should be.

This shift aligns with conversations happening across fashion communities — from discussions about curating a wardrobe with intention to the growing interest in pieces that carry personal meaning. The afterparty aesthetic, in particular, thrives on this energy: bold, expressive, unapologetic, and deeply individual.

## Custom Clothing and the Streetwear Connection

Streetwear has always had a DIY spirit at its core. From the early days of hand-stenciled tees and custom sneaker modifications to today's sophisticated made-to-order drops, the culture has consistently celebrated creativity and self-expression over conformity. Custom clothing sits naturally within this tradition.

What's changed is accessibility. Digital platforms, social media communities, and independent artisans have made it easier than ever to commission or create custom pieces. You don't need to be a fashion insider to get something made specifically for you — you just need to know where to look and what you want.

The afterparty context is particularly fitting here. Post-event dressing — the outfit you wear when the main event is over and the real night begins — has its own set of rules. It's looser, more personal, more expressive. It's where custom pieces shine, because the setting rewards individuality rather than conformity.

## Why Custom Clothing Is Also a Sustainability Statement

Beyond aesthetics, choosing custom clothing carries real environmental and ethical weight. Fast fashion's impact on the planet is well-documented: overproduction, textile waste, exploitative labor practices, and a culture of disposability that treats clothing as single-use. Custom clothing pushes back against all of that.

When you commission or create a bespoke piece, you're investing in something made with intention — typically by a skilled artisan, using quality materials, in quantities that don't generate excess. You're more likely to wear it repeatedly, care for it properly, and keep it for years rather than seasons. That's a fundamentally different relationship with clothing, and it's one that the fashion industry is slowly being pushed toward by a more conscious consumer base.

The "Custom clothes r cool :)" sentiment isn't just about aesthetics — it's a quiet declaration of values. It says: I care about what I wear, where it comes from, and what it says about me.

## How to Get Into Custom Clothing

If you're curious about exploring custom pieces but don't know where to start, here are some practical entry points:

- **Commission from independent makers.** Platforms like Etsy, Instagram, and local craft markets are full of talented artisans offering custom embroidery, painting, tailoring, and more. Start with something small — a hand-embroidered patch, a custom-painted jacket, a made-to-measure pair of trousers.
- **Upcycle what you already own.** Custom doesn't always mean new. Transforming a thrifted blazer with embroidery, dyeing an old pair of jeans, or adding hardware to a basic bag are all forms of customization that cost relatively little and yield genuinely unique results.
- **Learn a skill.** Screen printing, hand-painting, basic tailoring, and embroidery are all learnable with patience and practice. The satisfaction of wearing something you made yourself is genuinely unmatched.
- **Support small brands with custom options.** Many independent fashion brands now offer made-to-order or customizable pieces. Choosing these over mass-market alternatives supports both artisanal craftsmanship and a more sustainable fashion ecosystem.

## Practical Tips for Styling Custom Pieces

- **Let the custom piece be the focal point.** If you're wearing a hand-painted jacket or a heavily embroidered top, keep the rest of the outfit simple. The piece should speak for itself.
- **Mix custom with classic basics.** A custom statement piece looks strongest against a clean backdrop — white tee, straight-leg jeans, simple sneakers. The contrast makes the custom element pop.
- **Don't over-coordinate.** Custom clothing works best when it feels organic, not overly styled. Wear it the way you'd wear a favorite vintage find — naturally, confidently, without overthinking it.
- **Document it.** Custom pieces have stories. Photograph them, share them, talk about them. Part of what makes bespoke clothing meaningful is the narrative attached to it.

## Conclusion

The afterparty is where the real style happens — and custom clothing is perfectly suited to that energy. As fashion continues to evolve away from mass production and toward more personal, intentional choices, the appeal of bespoke garments will only grow. ## FAQ

**What is custom clothing and how is it different from regular fashion?**
Custom clothing refers to garments that are made to order, personalized, or created specifically for an individual — as opposed to mass-produced pieces available to everyone. This includes made-to-measure tailoring, hand-painted or embroidered items, upcycled pieces, and bespoke commissions from independent makers.

**Is custom clothing more sustainable than fast fashion?**
Generally, yes. Custom and bespoke pieces are typically made in smaller quantities, using better materials, by skilled artisans who are fairly compensated. They're also more likely to be worn repeatedly and cared for over time, reducing the waste associated with disposable fast fashion.

**How do I find someone to make custom clothing for me?**
Start with platforms like Etsy, Instagram, and local craft markets. Search for independent makers who specialize in the type of customization you're looking for — embroidery, tailoring, hand-painting, etc. Many makers also take commissions through direct message on social media.

**Can I customize clothes I already own?**
Absolutely. Upcycling existing pieces is one of the most accessible and sustainable forms of customization. Adding embroidery, patches, dye, hardware, or altered hems can completely transform something you already own into a genuinely unique piece.

**Why is custom clothing popular in streetwear culture?**
Streetwear has always celebrated individuality, creativity, and self-expression — values that align naturally with custom clothing. The DIY ethos at the heart of streetwear culture makes bespoke and personalized pieces a natural fit, from hand-painted sneakers to custom-embroidered hoodies.

On paper, Under Armour and Balenciaga shouldn't make sense together. ## Why the Casual Going-Out Look Has Become Fashion's Most Interesting Category

For a long time, "going out" meant dressing up. Heels, a fitted dress, something that signaled you'd made an effort. That's still a valid approach, but it's no longer the dominant one — particularly among a younger, fashion-forward demographic that has grown up seeing luxury and streetwear coexist on the same runways.

The casual going-out outfit has become fashion's most interesting category because it requires genuine skill to pull off. It's easy to look dressed up in a formal outfit. It's much harder to look intentionally casual in a way that reads as stylish rather than underdressed. The difference lies in the details: the quality of the fabrics, the precision of the fit, the coherence of the silhouette, and the confidence with which the look is worn.

The Under Armour x Balenciaga outfit nails all of these. The flared cargo pant has a deliberate, architectural quality. The 10XLs are unmistakably considered. The overall effect is relaxed but never sloppy — a distinction that defines the best casual going-out dressing.

## The Broader Shift: Comfort Meets Sophistication

The pandemic permanently altered how people think about clothing. Years of working from home, avoiding formal occasions, and prioritizing comfort over convention have left a lasting mark on consumer preferences. The demand for clothing that conveys both sophistication and ease has surged — and it hasn't retreated.

This shift is visible across every segment of the fashion market. Luxury brands are producing more relaxed silhouettes. Streetwear labels are investing in higher-quality materials. The middle ground — where comfort and elegance genuinely coexist — has become the most commercially and culturally significant space in fashion.

The casual going-out outfit sits squarely in that middle ground. It's the category that best reflects where fashion actually is right now: less formal, more personal, and increasingly defined by how a garment feels to wear rather than just how it looks.

## How to Build Your Own Casual Going-Out Look

You don't need a Balenciaga budget to apply these principles. The key is understanding what makes the look work and translating that into pieces at whatever price point suits you.

**Focus on silhouette first.** The Under Armour x Balenciaga outfit works because the proportions are deliberate and consistent. If you're wearing wide-leg or flared trousers, make sure the top and footwear are chosen with that volume in mind. Proportion is everything in casual dressing — get it right and the look feels intentional; get it wrong and it just looks like you grabbed whatever was clean.

**Invest in one statement piece.** You don't need every item to be luxury. One genuinely great piece — a well-constructed trouser, a quality sneaker, a considered outerwear item — can anchor an entire outfit and elevate everything around it.

**Keep the palette cohesive.** Casual going-out outfits tend to work best in a limited color range. Tonal dressing — varying shades of the same color family — is particularly effective and easy to execute. Monochrome looks in grey, black, navy, or earth tones are reliable starting points.

**Let the shoes do work.** Footwear is often the most expressive element of a casual outfit. A considered sneaker, a quality boot, or an unexpected shoe choice can transform a simple ensemble into something genuinely memorable.

**Wear it with confidence.** This is the non-negotiable. Casual luxury dressing requires a certain ease of carriage — an unselfconsciousness that communicates you're comfortable in what you're wearing. That confidence is ultimately what makes the look land.

## Practical Tips

- Flared and wide-leg trousers are having a strong moment — invest in a quality pair that fits well through the seat and thigh, with the flare starting at the knee.
- Oversized sneakers work best when the rest of the outfit is relatively streamlined. If both the trousers and the shoes are voluminous, make sure the top half is fitted to balance the proportions.
- For going-out occasions, elevate a casual outfit with considered accessories: a quality watch, a simple chain, or a structured bag adds polish without formality.
- Avoid over-accessorizing. The strength of the casual going-out look lies in its restraint. One or two well-chosen accessories are more effective than several competing ones.

## Conclusion

The casual going-out outfit is one of fashion's most nuanced and rewarding categories. Done well — as the Under Armour x Balenciaga look demonstrates — it communicates sophistication, confidence, and a genuine understanding of contemporary style. The key is intentionality: every piece chosen with purpose, every proportion considered, every detail deliberate. That's what separates a great casual outfit from simply wearing comfortable clothes.

## What Makes a Three-Piece Outfit Work

The appeal of the three-piece outfit formula lies in its built-in coherence. Rather than assembling an outfit piece by piece and hoping everything works together, you start with a defined combination — in this case, shorts, a top, and footwear — and make considered choices within that framework. The result tends to look more intentional than a random assembly of individual pieces, even when the individual items are relatively simple.

What makes this particular formula especially strong is the versatility of its anchor piece. The It shorts of summer 2026 — characterized by a tailored, slightly high-waisted cut with a relaxed leg opening — work across a surprisingly wide range of occasions. They're polished enough for a lunch out, casual enough for a beach day, and interesting enough to anchor an evening look when paired with the right top and shoes.

The three-piece structure also simplifies the dressing process in a way that's genuinely useful. In a world where decision fatigue is real and wardrobes are often overstuffed with pieces that don't work together, having a reliable formula you can return to repeatedly is a genuine asset.

## The It Shorts: What They Are and Why They Work

The shorts at the center of this trend occupy an interesting middle ground between tailored and relaxed. They're not the ultra-short, body-conscious styles that dominated a few seasons ago, nor are they the baggy, utilitarian cargo shorts that have been circulating in streetwear. They sit somewhere in between: a mid-thigh to above-knee length, a slightly high-waisted cut that elongates the leg, and a clean, minimal design that reads as polished rather than casual.

This silhouette works for several reasons. The higher waist creates a flattering proportion regardless of body type. The relaxed leg opening is comfortable in warm weather without looking sloppy. And the clean, minimal design means they pair easily with a wide range of tops — from a simple fitted tee to a more structured blouse — without competing for visual attention.

Zara's version of this silhouette has been particularly well-received, which speaks to the brand's ability to identify and execute on emerging trends at an accessible price point. But the formula works regardless of where the shorts come from — the key is the cut and the proportion, not the label.

## Building the Three-Piece Formula

**The Shorts**
As established, the It shorts are the anchor. Choose a cut that flatters your proportions — mid-thigh to above-knee length, slightly high-waisted, with a clean minimal design. Neutral tones (white, ecru, tan, black) are the most versatile; a bold color or subtle print can work as a statement choice.

**The Top**
The top is where you have the most room to express your personal style. A few combinations that work particularly well with this shorts silhouette:

- A fitted ribbed tank in a tonal or contrasting color — simple, clean, and effortlessly cool
- A lightweight linen or cotton button-down, worn open over a simple bralette or tucked in at the front
- A structured crop top with an interesting neckline — square, asymmetric, or off-shoulder — that adds visual interest without competing with the shorts
- A simple white tee, slightly oversized, tucked loosely at the front for a relaxed but considered look

The key is to maintain the proportion balance: if the shorts are relaxed, keep the top fitted; if the shorts are more tailored, you can afford a slightly looser top.

**The Footwear**
Footwear is what elevates this formula from casual to genuinely chic. The most effective choices this season include:

- A strappy flat sandal in a neutral or metallic tone — clean, versatile, and effortlessly elegant
- A low-heeled mule or slide that adds a touch of polish without sacrificing comfort
- A quality white sneaker for a more casual, sporty interpretation of the look
- A pointed-toe flat in leather or suede for an evening-appropriate version

The sandal-with-white-dress principle applies here too: the right footwear can transform a simple outfit into something that looks genuinely editorial.
