Casual Going Out Fit: How Luxury Streetwear Is Redefining After-Dark Dressing

OThe Stylist Suitcase/Style Shopping

May 25, 2026 - 7 min read

The line between luxury fashion and streetwear has been blurring for years — but what we're seeing now goes beyond a simple crossover. It's a full-on merger, and nowhere is it more visible than in the casual going-out outfit. The kind of look that says you put thought into it without looking like you tried too hard. The kind that works equally well at a gallery opening, a late-night dinner, or the afterparty that follows.

A recent outfit breakdown featuring the Under Armour x Balenciaga collaboration — specifically the Balenciaga George V Flared Cargos paired with Balenciaga 10XLs — captures this moment perfectly. It's something simple, as the original post notes. But "simple" here is doing a lot of work.

The Under Armour x Balenciaga Collaboration: What It Represents

On paper, Under Armour and Balenciaga shouldn't make sense together. One is a performance sportswear brand built around athletic function; the other is a Paris-based luxury house known for pushing fashion to its conceptual limits. But that tension is precisely what makes the collaboration interesting.

Balenciaga has long been fascinated by the aesthetics of the everyday — workwear, athletic gear, utilitarian silhouettes — and elevating them into something that feels genuinely luxurious without abandoning their functional roots. The George V Flared Cargos are a perfect example: a cargo trouser, a staple of both workwear and streetwear, reimagined with Balenciaga's signature oversized proportions and impeccable construction.

Pairing them with the 10XLs — Balenciaga's deliberately exaggerated sneaker silhouette — creates a look that's cohesive in its commitment to scale and volume. Everything is big, everything is intentional, and the result reads as sophisticated rather than sloppy.

Why the Casual Going-Out Look Has Become Fashion's Most Interesting Category

For a long time, "going out" meant dressing up. Heels, a fitted dress, something that signaled you'd made an effort. That's still a valid approach, but it's no longer the dominant one — particularly among a younger, fashion-forward demographic that has grown up seeing luxury and streetwear coexist on the same runways.

The casual going-out outfit has become fashion's most interesting category because it requires genuine skill to pull off. It's easy to look dressed up in a formal outfit. It's much harder to look intentionally casual in a way that reads as stylish rather than underdressed. The difference lies in the details: the quality of the fabrics, the precision of the fit, the coherence of the silhouette, and the confidence with which the look is worn.

The Under Armour x Balenciaga outfit nails all of these. The flared cargo pant has a deliberate, architectural quality. The 10XLs are unmistakably considered. The overall effect is relaxed but never sloppy — a distinction that defines the best casual going-out dressing.

The Broader Shift: Comfort Meets Sophistication

The pandemic permanently altered how people think about clothing. Years of working from home, avoiding formal occasions, and prioritizing comfort over convention have left a lasting mark on consumer preferences. The demand for clothing that conveys both sophistication and ease has surged — and it hasn't retreated.

This shift is visible across every segment of the fashion market. Luxury brands are producing more relaxed silhouettes. Streetwear labels are investing in higher-quality materials. The middle ground — where comfort and elegance genuinely coexist — has become the most commercially and culturally significant space in fashion.

The casual going-out outfit sits squarely in that middle ground. It's the category that best reflects where fashion actually is right now: less formal, more personal, and increasingly defined by how a garment feels to wear rather than just how it looks.

How to Build Your Own Casual Going-Out Look

You don't need a Balenciaga budget to apply these principles. The key is understanding what makes the look work and translating that into pieces at whatever price point suits you.

Focus on silhouette first. The Under Armour x Balenciaga outfit works because the proportions are deliberate and consistent. If you're wearing wide-leg or flared trousers, make sure the top and footwear are chosen with that volume in mind. Proportion is everything in casual dressing — get it right and the look feels intentional; get it wrong and it just looks like you grabbed whatever was clean.

Invest in one statement piece. You don't need every item to be luxury. One genuinely great piece — a well-constructed trouser, a quality sneaker, a considered outerwear item — can anchor an entire outfit and elevate everything around it.

Keep the palette cohesive. Casual going-out outfits tend to work best in a limited color range. Tonal dressing — varying shades of the same color family — is particularly effective and easy to execute. Monochrome looks in grey, black, navy, or earth tones are reliable starting points.

Let the shoes do work. Footwear is often the most expressive element of a casual outfit. A considered sneaker, a quality boot, or an unexpected shoe choice can transform a simple ensemble into something genuinely memorable.

Wear it with confidence. This is the non-negotiable. Casual luxury dressing requires a certain ease of carriage — an unselfconsciousness that communicates you're comfortable in what you're wearing. That confidence is ultimately what makes the look land.

Practical Tips

  • Flared and wide-leg trousers are having a strong moment — invest in a quality pair that fits well through the seat and thigh, with the flare starting at the knee.
  • Oversized sneakers work best when the rest of the outfit is relatively streamlined. If both the trousers and the shoes are voluminous, make sure the top half is fitted to balance the proportions.
  • For going-out occasions, elevate a casual outfit with considered accessories: a quality watch, a simple chain, or a structured bag adds polish without formality.
  • Avoid over-accessorizing. The strength of the casual going-out look lies in its restraint. One or two well-chosen accessories are more effective than several competing ones.

Conclusion

The casual going-out outfit is one of fashion's most nuanced and rewarding categories. Done well — as the Under Armour x Balenciaga look demonstrates — it communicates sophistication, confidence, and a genuine understanding of contemporary style. The key is intentionality: every piece chosen with purpose, every proportion considered, every detail deliberate. That's what separates a great casual outfit from simply wearing comfortable clothes.

FAQ

What makes a casual going-out outfit look intentional rather than underdressed? The key factors are proportion, quality, and coherence. Deliberate silhouettes, well-chosen footwear, and a limited color palette signal that the look was put together with thought. Confidence in how you wear it matters just as much as the pieces themselves.

Is the Balenciaga x Under Armour collaboration worth the investment? For those who genuinely love the aesthetic and wear the pieces regularly, yes. Balenciaga's construction quality is high, and the collaboration pieces have a distinctive visual identity that holds up over time. That said, the principles behind the look — considered proportions, quality basics, expressive footwear — can be applied at any price point.

How do you style flared cargo pants for a going-out occasion? Keep the top half fitted or streamlined to balance the volume of the flare. Pair with a quality sneaker or boot that complements the silhouette. Stick to a cohesive color palette and avoid over-accessorizing — let the trouser be the focal point.

What's the difference between streetwear and luxury streetwear? Traditional streetwear prioritizes cultural relevance, graphic expression, and community identity. Luxury streetwear adds elevated materials, refined construction, and the prestige of high-fashion branding. The best luxury streetwear retains the energy and authenticity of its roots while adding genuine quality.

Can you dress casually for a night out without looking underdressed? Absolutely — and increasingly, the most stylish people do exactly that. The trick is to choose pieces with clear intentionality: quality fabrics, considered proportions, and at least one element that signals you've thought about the look. A great sneaker, a well-cut trouser, or a distinctive outerwear piece can all do that job.
